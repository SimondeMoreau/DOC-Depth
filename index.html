DOC-Depth: A novel approach for dense depth ground truth generation

Simon de Moreau1,2, Mathias Corsia3, Hassan Bouchiba3,
Yasser Almehio2, Andrei Bursuc2, Hafid El-Idrissi2, Fabien Moutarde1,
1Mines Paris - PSL University, 2Valeo, 3Exwayz Research
Code Dataset

Abstract

Accurate depth information is essential for many computer vision applications. Yet, no available dataset recording method allows for fully dense accurate depth estimation in a large scale dynamic environment. In this paper, we introduce DOC-Depth, a novel efficient and easy-to-deploy approach for dense depth generation from any LiDAR sensor. After reconstructing consistent dense 3D environment using LiDAR odometry, we address dynamic objects occlusions automatically thanks to DOC, our state-of-the art dynamic object classification method. Additionally, our method is fast and scalable, allowing for the creation of unbounded datasets in terms of size and time. We demonstrate the effectiveness of our approach on the KITTI dataset, improving its density from 16.1% to 71.2% and release this new fully dense depth annotation, to facilitate future research in the domain. We also demonstrate results using various sensors and in multiple environments.
